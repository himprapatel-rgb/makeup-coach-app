# Content Research for Use-What-You-Have Makeup Coach

## Executive Summary

The Use-What-You-Have Makeup Coach requires comprehensive educational content to guide users from beginner to advanced makeup artists. This research document outlines content strategy, tutorial structure, skill progression pathways, and localization considerations for a global audience.

Key content areas: tutorial taxonomy, skill progression levels, video content strategy, written guides, interactive exercises, and multi-language localization.

---

## 1. Content Strategy Overview

### 1.1 Content Pillars
1. **Foundation Skills**: Basics (skin prep, primer, foundation, concealer)
2. **Color Theory**: Understanding undertones, complementary colors, seasonal palettes
3. **Technique Mastery**: Application methods, blending, contouring, highlighting
4. **Look Creation**: Complete eye looks, face looks, occasion-specific makeup
5. **Personalization**: Makeup for different skin tones, face shapes, age groups
6. **Problem Solving**: Covering blemishes, correcting color, fixing mistakes
7. **Inclusivity & Accessibility**: Makeup for disabilities, different skin conditions

### 1.2 Content Distribution

| Format | Purpose | Frequency | Examples |
|--------|---------|-----------|----------|
| Video tutorials | Step-by-step visual guidance | 3-5/week | "Foundation application for beginners" |
| Written guides | Quick reference, accessibility | 2-3/week | "Blending techniques explained" |
| Interactive quizzes | Knowledge assessment | 1/week | "Color theory quiz" |
| Live sessions | Community engagement | 1/month | "Q&A with makeup artist" |
| User challenges | Engagement, retention | 2/month | "Try this eyeshadow look" |

## 2. Tutorial Taxonomy & Structure

### 2.1 Content Categories

**Skill Level Hierarchy**:
1. **Level 1 - Beginner** (Weeks 1-4)
   - "What is makeup?"
   - Skin care basics before makeup
   - Foundation shade matching
   - Basic concealer application

2. **Level 2 - Intermediate** (Weeks 5-12)
   - Color theory fundamentals
   - Contour and highlight basics
   - Eyeshadow blending techniques
   - Lip color selection

3. **Level 3 - Advanced** (Weeks 13-26)
   - Advanced color correction
   - Complex eye looks
   - Art-based makeup design
   - Makeup for special occasions

4. **Level 4 - Expert** (Ongoing)
   - Professional makeup artistry
   - Specialty effects (bruising, scars)
   - Editorial makeup trends
   - Makeup for film/theater

### 2.2 Tutorial Structure Template

**Every tutorial should include**:
- Introduction (30 seconds): What you'll learn, difficulty level
- Materials list: Products, brushes, tools needed
- Step-by-step instructions (with visual breaks every 2 minutes)
- Common mistakes section
- Variations/alternatives
- Quick quiz or assessment
- Related tutorial links

**Recommended video length**:
- Beginner: 5-8 minutes
- Intermediate: 8-12 minutes
- Advanced: 12-15 minutes
- Expert: 15-20 minutes

### 2.3 Content Progression Path

```
Week 1-2: Skin Prep & Primer
    ↓
Week 3-4: Foundation Basics
    ↓
Week 5-6: Concealer & Correction
    ↓
Week 7-8: Color Theory
    ↓
Week 9-10: Eyeshadow Application
    ↓
Week 11-12: Contour & Highlight
    ↓
Week 13-16: Complete Looks (Easy)
    ↓
Week 17-20: Complete Looks (Advanced)
    ↓
Week 21+: Specialization Tracks
```

## 3. Video Content Strategy

### 3.1 Video Production Specifications

| Element | Specification | Rationale |
|---------|---------------|-----------|
| Resolution | 1080p minimum (4K optional) | High quality for makeup detail visibility |
| Frame rate | 30fps or 60fps | Smooth motion for detailed techniques |
| Lighting | Professional key + fill lights | Even illumination on face |
| Audio | High-quality lavalier microphone | Clear audio for instruction |
| Captions | Burnt-in + SRT file | Accessibility for deaf users |
| Color grading | Neutral, accurate skin tones | Accurate makeup color representation |

### 3.2 Video Series Recommendation

**Series 1: "Makeup Fundamentals"** (26 episodes)
- Duration: 5-8 minutes each
- Target: Complete beginners
- Content: Week-by-week progression

**Series 2: "Mastering Techniques"** (20 episodes)
- Duration: 10-12 minutes each
- Target: Intermediate users
- Content: In-depth technique breakdowns

**Series 3: "Creating Looks"** (40 episodes)
- Duration: 12-15 minutes each
- Target: Intermediate to advanced
- Content: Specific makeup looks (by occasion, season, trend)

**Series 4: "Advanced Artistry"** (30 episodes)
- Duration: 15-20 minutes each
- Target: Advanced users
- Content: Creative makeup, special effects, editorial looks

### 3.3 Video Publishing Calendar

| Season | Focus | Content Themes |
|--------|-------|-----------------|
| Q1 | Spring Beauty | Fresh looks, pastels, dewy skin |
| Q2 | Summer Trends | Bold colors, long-wearing, SPF awareness |
| Q3 | Fall Aesthetics | Warm tones, bronze, earth tones |
| Q4 | Holiday/Winter | Bold lips, dramatic eyes, glamorous looks |

## 4. Written Content & Guides

### 4.1 Written Content Types

1. **Quick Guides** (500-1000 words)
   - "How to pick your foundation shade"
   - "Understanding undertones"
   - "5 must-have brushes for beginners"

2. **In-Depth Articles** (1500-3000 words)
   - "Complete guide to contouring for different face shapes"
   - "Color theory for makeup: A scientific approach"
   - "Makeup for mature skin: Comprehensive guide"

3. **Checklists & Templates**
   - "Pre-makeup skincare checklist"
   - "Makeup inventory template"
   - "Makeup kit essentials list"

4. **FAQs**
   - "Why does my foundation look patchy?"
   - "How do I fix a makeup mistake?"
   - "What makeup works for sensitive skin?"

| "Contouring tutorial" | High | Medium | Video series |

## 5. Interactive Content & Exercises

### 5.1 Learning Engagement Tools

**Interactive Quizzes**
- Topic: Color theory
- Questions: 10 multiple choice + image-based questions
- Completion time: 5 minutes
- Feedback: Immediate with explanations

**Skill Assessment Tests**
- Pre-assessment: "What's your makeup skill level?"
- Mid-assessment: After 6 weeks of tutorials
- Post-assessment: After 12 weeks of learning
- Adaptive: Recommend next level based on score

**Makeup Challenges**
- Weekly prompt-based challenges (e.g., "Create a look using only 3 products")
- Bi-weekly trend challenges ("Recreate this celebrity look")
- Monthly masterpiece challenge (open-ended creative challenge)
- Community voting on best submissions

### 5.2 Exercise Structure

Each skill module should include:
1. Learning video (7-10 min)
2. Practice exercise (with photo feedback)
3. Quiz (5-10 questions)
4. Challenge variation (harder version)

## 6. Skill Progression & Personalization

### 6.1 Personalization Pathways

| User Profile | Recommended Path | Duration | Focus |
|--------------|------------------|----------|-------|
| Complete beginner | Full curriculum | 24 weeks | Foundations |
| Experienced makeup wearer | Intermediate+ | 12 weeks | Advanced techniques |
| Professional makeup artist | Advanced + Expert | 8 weeks | Specialization |
| Special needs | Modified curriculum | Variable | Accessibility features |

### 6.2 User Customization Options

Users should be able to select:
- **Skin tone**: Accurate shade matching and technique demonstrations
- **Skin type**: Oily, dry, combination, sensitive
- **Face shape**: Contouring and highlighting recommendations
- **Interests**: Focus areas (eye makeup, lips, contouring, etc.)
- **Learning style**: Visual, kinesthetic, reading/writing
- **Time availability**: 5-min quick tips vs. in-depth tutorials

## 7. Content for Special Populations

### 7.1 Inclusive Makeup Content

**By Skin Tone**:
- Separate shade recommendations for cool, neutral, warm undertones
- Demonstrations on models with diverse skin tones (at least 6+ shades)
- Specific content for dark skin, medium skin, light skin
- Product recommendations for different skin tones

**By Face Shape**:
- Content for oval, round, square, heart, oblong faces
- Contouring guides specific to each shape
- Placement guidance for blush, highlight, bronzer

**By Age**:
- Makeup for teens (acne coverage, trends)
- Makeup for 20s-30s (career, events)
- Makeup for 40s+ (mature skin, anti-aging techniques)
- Makeup for aging well (celebrating natural features)

**By Ability**:
- Makeup for arthritis (adaptive brush designs)
- Makeup for limited vision (high-contrast tutorials)
- Makeup for tremors (stabilization techniques)
- One-handed makeup application techniques

### 7.2 Accessibility Features

| Feature | Implementation |
|---------|-----------------|
| Captions | All videos with full captions + SRT files |
| Audio descriptions | Key visual steps described verbally |
| High contrast | Option for high-contrast video mode |
| Text size | Adjustable text size for guides |
| Color alternatives | Descriptions beyond color (warm, cool, sheer, opaque) |
| Speed adjustment | 0.5x to 2x video playback speed |

## 8. Content Localization Strategy

### 8.1 Localization Priority Languages

| Language | Market | Priority | Timeline |
|----------|--------|----------|----------|
| English | US, UK, India | Phase 1 | Month 1-3 |
| Spanish | Mexico, Spain, LA | Phase 2 | Month 4-6 |
| Mandarin | China, Taiwan | Phase 2 | Month 4-6 |
| Hindi | India | Phase 3 | Month 7-9 |
| Portuguese | Brazil | Phase 3 | Month 7-9 |
| French | France, Canada | Phase 3 | Month 7-9 |

### 8.2 Localization Beyond Translation

**Cultural Adaptation**:
- Makeup trends by region (different looks popular in different countries)
- Product availability by region (what's actually sold in each market)
- Skin concerns by climate (humidity in tropics vs. dry climates)
- Festival/occasion-specific makeup (Diwali, Lunar New Year, etc.)

**Regional Content Creators**:
- Partner with local makeup artists for regional content
- Showcase regional makeup trends and traditions
- Feature beauty brands popular in each region
- Adapt tutorials for popular local makeup products
