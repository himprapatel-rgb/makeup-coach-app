## 2. User Personas

### Persona 1: The Overwhelmed Beginner

**Name:** Maya  
**Age:** 22  
**Occupation:** Recent college graduate  
**Tech Comfort:** High  
**Makeup Experience:** Beginner

#### Profile
- Has accumulated makeup products from gifts and impulse purchases
- Watches YouTube tutorials but can't replicate the looks
- Feels intimidated by complex techniques
- Wants to look professional for job interviews

#### Goals
- Learn basic everyday makeup
- Use products she already owns
- Build confidence in application
- Develop a quick morning routine

#### Pain Points
- Doesn't know where to start
- Products sit unused because she doesn't know how to use them
- Tutorials move too fast or use products she doesn't have
- Embarrassed to ask for help in stores

#### Quote
> "I have a drawer full of makeup I bought because it looked good on influencers, but I have no idea how to use any of it on my face."

---

### Persona 2: The Skill Stagnator

**Name:** Priya  
**Age:** 34  
**Occupation:** Marketing Manager  
**Tech Comfort:** Medium-High  
**Makeup Experience:** Intermediate

#### Profile
- Has a set routine but wants to expand her skills
- Comfortable with basics but struggles with advanced techniques
- Interested in learning contouring, eye looks, and occasion makeup
- Doesn't have time for long tutorials

#### Goals
- Master specific techniques (contouring, smoky eye)
- Learn to adapt looks for different occasions
- Reduce her product collection to essentials
- Save time with efficient routines

#### Pain Points
- Stuck in a makeup "rut" with the same look every day
- Intimidated by complex tutorials
- Bought products for looks she never learned to create
- Lacks personalized feedback on her technique

#### Quote
> "I've been doing the same makeup for 5 years. I want to level up but don't know which tutorials are actually for my skill level."

### Persona 3: The Inclusive Seeker

**Name:** Aisha  
**Age:** 28  
**Occupation:** Nurse  
**Tech Comfort:** Medium  
**Makeup Experience:** Beginner-Intermediate

#### Profile
- Has darker skin tone underserved by mainstream tutorials
- Struggles to find products and techniques that work for her
- Frustrated by AI tools that don't recognize her features accurately
- Wants representation and personalized advice

#### Goals
- Find techniques that work for her skin tone
- Learn to color-match without trial and error
- See tutorials featuring people who look like her
- Build a routine that enhances her natural features

#### Pain Points
- 53% of Black consumers report difficulty finding matching products
- AI tools perform poorly on darker skin
- Most tutorials feature lighter skin tones
- Has wasted money on wrong shades

#### Quote
> "Every makeup AI tells me I'm a different shade. I just want something that actually sees me and works for my skin."

### Persona 4: The Sustainability-Conscious User

**Name:** Emma  
**Age:** 26  
**Occupation:** Environmental Consultant  
**Tech Comfort:** High  
**Makeup Experience:** Intermediate

#### Profile

### Persona 5: The Mature Learner

**Name:** Linda  
**Age:** 52  
**Occupation:** Real Estate Agent  
**Tech Comfort:** Low-Medium  
**Makeup Experience:** Intermediate (outdated techniques)

#### Profile
- Has been doing makeup the same way for decades
- Techniques she learned don't work on aging skin
- Feels excluded from trend-focused beauty content
- Wants age-appropriate, professional looks

## 4. User Journey Mapping

### 4.1 Current State Journey (Frustrated User)

```
Stage 1: AWARENESS
- Sees makeup look on social media
- Feels inspired to try it
- Emotion: Excited, curious

Stage 2: RESEARCH
- Searches for tutorials
- Finds overwhelming options
- Can't find tutorial for her specific face/products
- Emotion: Confused, overwhelmed

Stage 3: ATTEMPT
- Tries to follow tutorial
- Tutorial uses different products
- Technique doesn't translate to her face
- Emotion: Frustrated, inadequate

Stage 4: FAILURE
- Result doesn't match expectation
- Washes off makeup
- Products go back in drawer unused
- Emotion: Disappointed, defeated

Stage 5: CYCLE REPEATS
- Buys new products hoping for different result
- Same outcome
- Emotion: Hopeless, wasteful
```

### 4.2 Desired State Journey (With Makeup Coach)

```
Stage 1: ONBOARDING
- Scans existing products into "My Kit"
- Completes face analysis
- Sets skill level and goals
- Emotion: Empowered, hopeful

Stage 2: PERSONALIZED LEARNING
- Receives tutorials based on HER products
- Step-by-step guidance at her pace
- Techniques adapted for her face shape
- Emotion: Confident, supported

Stage 3: PRACTICE
- Follows along with real-time feedback
- Gets tips when struggling
- Sees progress compared to before
- Emotion: Improving, motivated

Stage 4: MASTERY
- Completes look successfully
- Saves to progress gallery
- Unlocks next skill level
- Emotion: Accomplished, proud

Stage 5: EXPANSION
- Applies skills to new looks
- Uses more of existing products
- Confidently tries variations
- Emotion: Creative, independent
```

## 7. Research Recommendations

### 7.1 For Product Development

1. **Prioritize personalization** - Users want guidance specific to their face, products, and skill level
2. **Design for beginners** - Largest underserved segment with highest frustration
3. **Build inclusively from day one** - Don't retrofit accessibility
4. **Focus on skill-building, not sales** - Users are tired of commerce-first apps
5. **Enable progress tracking** - Users want to see improvement over time

### 7.2 For Marketing

1. **Lead with education** - Users research extensively before committing
2. **Leverage social proof** - 83% of Gen Z trust creator recommendations
3. **Emphasize sustainability** - 67.7% value eco-consciousness
4. **Show real results** - Not filtered or unrealistic transformations
5. **Feature diverse users** - Representation matters for trust

### 7.3 For UX Design

1. **Mobile-first** - Primary usage is on smartphones
2. **Quick onboarding** - Don't overwhelm new users
3. **Offline capability** - Users apply makeup without internet
4. **Low-light performance** - Bathroom lighting varies
5. **Simple sharing** - Social media integration for progress

---

## 8. Conclusion

Our target users are **digitally savvy but makeup-frustrated** individuals who:

- Own products they don't know how to use
- Want personalized, inclusive guidance
- Value skill-building over product pushing
- Expect transparency and authenticity
- Demand accessibility and representation

The Use-What-You-Have Makeup Coach addresses these needs by positioning as a **trusted coach** rather than a sales platform, focusing on **practical skill development** with the products users already own.
